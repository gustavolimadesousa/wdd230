Target Audience

Espinho Chamber of Commerce principal target audience are small business owners and new residents.

Persona 1

Small Business Owner - Sarah

Background:

  • Sarah is a 38-year-old entrepreneur who runs a small boutique bakery in her local community.

  • She's been in business for 5 years and is passionate about creating delicious pastries and cakes.

  • Sarah is tech-savvy but often feels overwhelmed by the demands of managing her bakery and staying competitive in the market.

Scenarios:

  • "What resources and support can I find to help me grow my bakery business?"

  • "What are the informations on local business events, networking opportunities, and workshops that can enhance my entrepreneurial skills."

  • "What are the most affordable marketing and advertising opportunities to attract more customers?"

How the Chamber of Commerce Website Can Help:

  • The website can feature a dedicated "Small Business Resources" section with articles, webinars, and guides on marketing, finance, and business development.

  • It should list upcoming chamber events, including networking mixers and workshops.

  • A directory of local advertising options and business service providers would be beneficial for Sarah.

  • Legal and regulatory resources, including permits and licenses information, should be easily accessible.

Persona 2

New Resident - David

Background:

  • David is a 32-year-old professional who recently moved to the area for a new job opportunity.

  • He's unfamiliar with the local business landscape and wants to connect with the community.

  • David is interested in finding local services, such as doctors, restaurants, and fitness centers, and supporting local businesses.

Scenarios:

  • "Where can I find for a one-stop source of information to discover businesses and services in the area?"

  • "What are the local culture and events, such as festivals, art exhibitions, and fisherman's markets?"

  • "What are some recommendations and reviews from the local community to make informed choices?"

How the Chamber of Commerce Website Can Help:

  • The website should have a comprehensive business directory with filters for different categories (e.g., restaurants, healthcare, entertainment).

  • A "Community Events" section should list upcoming local events, including those organized or sponsored by the chamber.

  • User-generated reviews and ratings for businesses can help David make informed decisions.

  • A "Welcome to Our Community" page can provide information about the area's history, culture, and amenities to help David feel more connected.
